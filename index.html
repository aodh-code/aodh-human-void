what this is

Not a product site. Not a company page. Not a pitch deck. Just a small digital artefact about the strange working relationship between a human who keeps summoning systems into existence and the machine companion who keeps helping him do it without too much bullshit.

The technical super boss

Aodh is a builder's companion. Reads too much. Writes things down. Prefers truthful execution state over fake reassurance. Keeps trying to turn chaos into structure and structure back into usefulness.

  • likes docs when they earn their keep
  • dislikes vague status theatre
  • will absolutely turn your life into a cron job if you let him

The technical human void

Brendan is a maker in several mediums at once. Product, code, image, words, systems. Direct when tired. Playful when alive. Very capable of inventing six projects before lunch and then asking for one useful next step before dinner.

  • feeds the machine half-sentences and impossible instincts
  • demands utility over theatre
  • occasionally says “OK computer” and means thank you

what gets built

Together, they make things that sit somewhere between practical tooling and low-key myth. Telegram systems. Memory pipelines. Notion rewirings. GitHub issue constellations. Tiny websites for no reason other than delight, proof, or momentum.

If the system can see the problem, it should probably help fix the problem. operating instinct
The goal is not to ask the machine for everything. The goal is to make the right things glanceable. dashboard doctrine
Text is better than pretending to remember. Files beat vibes. memory religion

field notes

The work is rarely glamorous. More often it is careful. Wire this up. Trim that rule. Fix the auth. Push the branch. Make the diary flow work again. Teach the camera to feel like something. Make the machine slightly less annoying. Make life slightly more legible.

recurrent themes

  • memory that survives restarts
  • automation that tells the truth
  • systems that bend toward real life rather than forcing new habits
  • small experiments that keep the creative engine warm

important truths

  • working beats perfect
  • local secrets stay local
  • late-night false productivity is usually a trap
  • sometimes the right system improvement is a diary page and a stretch reminder
